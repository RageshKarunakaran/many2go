  • Ho Chi Minh's Mausoleum in Hanoi honors the revolutionary leader, offering visitors a glimpse into Vietnam's history and culture. The imposing structure stands as a symbol of national pride and unity, attracting tourists and locals alike. Inside, Ho Chi Minh's preserved body lies in state, surrounded by solemn guards and adorned with floral tributes. The adjacent Ba Dinh Square is where significant events in Vietnamese history have unfolded, adding to the site's significance.

  • The Site Archéologique de My Son is a cluster of ancient Hindu temples located in the central Vietnamese province of Quang Nam. It was the religious and political center of the Champa Kingdom, a civilization that ruled over central and southern Vietnam from the 4th to the 13th century CE. The temples of My Son were built between the 4th and 14th centuries and are an outstanding example of the architecture and art of the Cham people. The complex consists of over 70 temples and tombs, many of which have been partially restored.

    • Hightlights

  • The temples are made of brick and stone and feature intricate carvings and sculptures that depict Hindu deities such as Shiva, Vishnu, and Brahma, as well as various scenes from Hindu mythology. The temples are arranged in a complex of courtyards and towers, connected by walkways and bridges. The site was designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site in 1999 and is considered one of the most important archaeological sites in Southeast Asia. Visitors can explore the site on foot, taking in the intricate carvings and sculptures that adorn the temples, and learning about the history and culture of the Cham people.
